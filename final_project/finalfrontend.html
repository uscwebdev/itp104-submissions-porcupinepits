Welcome to Johnny Ving's Portfolio

Greetings! I'm Johnny Ving, currently a third-year student at the University of Southern California, pursuing a major in Computer Science with a focus on Game Design. My academic journey began at Evergreen Valley College, where I laid the foundation in computer science. This path led me to USC, where I'm now diving deep into the intricate world of game design and development.

Beyond academia, I'm actively involved in the gaming industry as a content creator at Fire Games, working under the renowned creator Preston. Here, I'm harnessing my creativity and technical skills to contribute to engaging and innovative game content. This role complements my academic pursuits, allowing me to apply theoretical knowledge in a real-world setting, thus enriching my understanding of the gaming landscape.

This portfolio is a window into my world – a blend of academic achievements, professional experiences, and a deep-seated passion for game development. Join me in exploring this dynamic and exciting journey.

Projects

Tubby's World

Unreleased project. Estimated to be released in December 2023. Follow on Twitter: https://twitter.com/TubbyWorld

Escape Skibi Obby

A silly project we worked on with the Fire Games team over the timespan of a week. In response to a "Skibidi" challenge by Little Big, we created a game that was inspired by the music video. Find this here: https://www.roblox.com/games/13884735771/Escape-Skibi-Obby

100 Laws

Project developed in 2 weeks as content for Preston video. Explore a satirical world made by poking fun at a lot of the top performing games on Roblox. We collaborated with permission of the owners of the original games and made 'meme' versions of each. Explore this huge world and break as many stupid laws as possible in order to gain achievements!

Songify

This game was developed in 2016, when I was only 13-14 years old. Songify is a competitive Guess The Song game, where you go up against other players to see who really knows their music! It consists of several different types of matches, such as team deathmatches, or even juggernaut rounds. This game performed very well at the time, but it died down as I left the platform for a while to pursue other interests.

Panik

This game went through 6 months of development. It was released and was a huge hit! Happy, the universe's first autonomous store-cleaning robot, seems to be programmed to do more than just eliminate waste... Can you be the first to escape Wobmart after dark? Or will you be the next victim of Happy's wrath? In this game you must find scanners & pick up items needed to unlock them, push other players to mess them up and take their items, and complete all 3 scanners to unlock trapdoors and escape.
